User Cases
Chatbots have a wide range of use cases, from managing and evolving chatbot content to co-developing chatbots with stakeholders to address specific needs. Here are some examples of chatbot use cases:
1. Chatbot Management: The Chatbot Management Process is a methodology for content management on chatbot systems that allows a cyclic and human-supervised process. This process is based on the analysis of user interactions and is divided into three distinct phases, namely, manage, build, and analyze.
2. Chatbot Management: Professional Journalism: Chatbots can be integrated into professional journalism to provide news organizations with new and innovative ways to create and share their content. Coding-free chatbot creation platforms are available to journalists today, and a three-phase evaluation framework can be used to evaluate the interactivity features that they offer to media industry workers.
3. University Institutional Page: Chatbots can be used as virtual assistants of a university institutional page. . They can interact through the Messenger application, from Facebook, and provide answers to demands for recurring information.
4. Mental Health and Wellbeing: Chatbots can be co-developed with stakeholders, such as young people, technology partners, and expert stakeholders, to address specific needs, such as mental health and wellbeing. They can communicate evidence-based resources, wellbeing support, educational mental health information, and adaptive coping strategies.
5. Web-based Audio Management: Chatbots can be used for content management and creation, such as web-based audio management. A functional multi-server chatbot-based architecture is presented with a multitrack audio editor as a web application. This architecture promotes a path of sharing, inclusion, and fosters a new way to establish audio communities with the help of chatbots.
