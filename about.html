Developing content for a chatbot is a crucial process that requires careful planning and execution. The content should be relevant, engaging, and informative to ensure a positive user experience. There are several approaches to managing chatbot content, such as the Chatbot Management Process, which is a methodology for content management on chatbot systems. This methodology is based on the analysis of user interactions, allowing a cyclic and human-supervised process. The proposed methodology is divided into three distinct phases, namely, manage, build, and analyze.
Another important factor in chatbot content development is the feasibility of chatbot programming by non-technical personnel using coding-free platforms and the usability of the created chatbot agents for news reporting to the audience. Therefore, it is essential to evaluate the interactivity features that chatbot creation platforms offer to media industry workers using an appropriate metrics framework.
In addition, chatbots can be co-developed with stakeholders, such as young people, technology partners, and expert stakeholders, to address specific needs, such as mental health and wellbeing. The co-design process with youth who are the key stakeholders to benefit from this digital tool can inform the creation of the chatbot's personality, character design, conversation design, and content development.
In summary, developing content for a chatbot requires careful planning and execution, and there are several approaches to managing chatbot content. It is essential to evaluate the interactivity features that chatbot creation platforms offer to media industry workers using an appropriate metrics framework. Co-designing chatbots with stakeholders can inform the creation of the chatbot's personality, character design, conversation design, and content development. Chatbots can also be used for content management and creation, such as web-based audio management and virtual assistant of a university institutional page.
